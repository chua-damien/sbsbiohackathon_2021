–A user-friendly interface for gene expression analysis on different Campylobacter jejuni morphotypes (Spiral VS Filamentous).–

1 - Research statement

1.1 - Background

The varying morphotype of bacteria encompasses distinct transcriptomic profiles. Our group has collated 51 experiments of varying morphotypes in Campylobacter jejuni (C.jejuni) and analyzed their associated differentially expressed genes.

1.2 - Research question

We intend to explore the difference in expression of possible virulence factors and pathways that could promote the survival of C.jejuni across different morphotypes.


2 - Methodology

A brief outline of the revised methodology is illustrated here:

2.1 - Acqusition and pseudoalignment of raw reads

We acquired raw reads from experiments that fulfills our research criteria (Spiral / Filamentous morphotypes). In total, 51 Fastq files were downloaded from ENA using the L-STRaP-cloud pipeline, limited to the first 1GB of each file. Subsequently, we pseudoaligned the raw RNA-seq reads based on C.jejuni(GCA_001877255::ASM187725v1) reference transcriptome with kallisto v0.46.1.50.

2.2 - Functional annotation of aligned reads

Functional annotation of each gene was conducted with Interproscan v5.51-85.0 to obtain gene ontology terms and gene function predction.

2.3 Differential gene expression analysis

Our group utilized the raw read counts obtained from pseudoalignment and conducted differential gene expression analysis using DESeq2 v1.30.1. Multi-factorial design was employed to study the effect of strain types and morphology on DGE. Finally, only genes that are differentially expressed were subjected to further analysis .
